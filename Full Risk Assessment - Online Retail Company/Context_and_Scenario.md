# Scenario:
 "NorthPeak Outfitters" is a small online retailer (25 employees) selling outdoor gear through a Shopify-based storefront, with a third-party payment gateway, an email marketing platform holding customer data, a cloud-hosted product/order database, remote customer service staff using personal laptops, and a warehouse fulfillment team using handheld scanners on the same Wi-Fi as the office. Leadership wants a full risk assessment before the Black Friday sales surge.

# Your Task : Full Risk Assessment Workflow
- Identify assets — list at least 6 (e.g., customer PII database, payment gateway integration, admin storefront panel, email marketing list, employee laptops, warehouse scanner network).

- Identify threats for each asset (e.g., credential-stuffing bots, phishing, insider error, DDoS during Black Friday traffic spike, third-party breach).
- Identify vulnerabilities (e.g., no MFA on admin panel, personal laptops without endpoint protection, flat network shared by POS/warehouse devices and guest Wi-Fi).
- Estimate likelihood (Rare/Unlikely/Possible/Likely/Almost Certain) for each risk — factor in the Black Friday traffic surge context.
- Estimate impact (Negligible/Minor/Moderate/Major/Severe) — think revenue loss, legal exposure, brand damage.
- Calculate risk level using the 5×5 matrix from Topic 11.
- Recommend controls for each risk, classified by type and function.
- Select a treatment option (Accept/Mitigate/Transfer/Avoid) with a one-sentence justification.
- Document everything
