I performed a risk assessment for a fictional online retailer preparing for Black Friday —
identifying assets, threats, and vulnerabilities, then rating each risk using a 5x5
likelihood x impact matrix.

Key lesson: a "High" risk isn't just a number ; two risks can land in the same box
for completely different reasons, and that changes how you'd treat them.

Also learned the difference between inherent risk (before controls) and residual risk
(after controls)  this is the number auditors actually ask for.




#Scenario:
"NorthPeak Outfitters" is a small online retailer (25 employees) selling outdoor gear through a Shopify-based storefront, with a third-party payment gateway,
an email marketing platform holding customer data, a cloud-hosted product/order database, remote customer service staff using personal laptops, and a warehouse
fulfillment team using handheld scanners on the same Wi-Fi as the office. Leadership wants a full risk assessment before the Black Friday sales surge.
